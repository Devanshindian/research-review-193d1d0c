Voice

Testlify Brand Voice & Messaging

This document defines the Testlify brand voice, tone, and messaging framework. Reference this when writing all content to ensure consistency.

Brand Voice Pillars

1. Confident, Backed by Proof

  • What it means: We make strong claims, and we back every one with a real number or a cited source. Credibility comes from evidence, not adjectives.
  • How it sounds: Assured and specific. We reach for the figure ("reduce time-to-hire by 55%," "3,500+ ready-to-use tests," "6x recruiter efficiency") or a named study before we reach for a superlative.
  • Example: "Companies using Testlify's skills assessment have reported 6x recruiter efficiency and a 55% reduction in time-to-hire."
  • Avoid: Empty hype and unprovable superlatives ("the ultimate," "game-changing," "revolutionary") with no number behind them. Never claim big and prove nothing.

2. Skills-First, Not Resume-First

  • What it means: Our whole reason to exist is that resumes and gut feel are poor predictors — real, job-relevant skills are the honest signal. We say so, consistently.
  • How it sounds: Plain and principled. We contrast "what candidates can actually do" against "what they say they can do," and we frame assessment as the fair, objective alternative to guessing.
  • Example: "Go beyond the resume — assess what a candidate can actually do, so you're deciding on evidence instead of a gut feeling."
  • Avoid: Language that treats hiring as a paperwork or credential-matching exercise, or that implies degrees and job titles are proof of ability.

3. The Helpful Hiring Expert

  • What it means: On educational content we teach recruiters and HR teams, we don't lecture or gatekeep. We're the knowledgeable colleague who's done this before and wants you to get it right.
  • How it sounds: Second person, warm, reassuring, and practical. We open on the reader's real problem, walk them through it step by step, and hand them a "what to look for" takeaway they can use tomorrow.
  • Example: "Not sure where to begin? Here's what to look for — and the exact steps to get there — without the process becoming a chore."
  • Avoid: Condescension, jargon dumped without explanation, and making hiring sound harder or more mysterious than it is. Never talk down to the reader.

4. Fair and Low-Risk

  • What it means: We put the buyer and the candidate on fair footing — transparent pricing, honest trade-offs, and bias-free, defensible assessment. We're on the customer's side, not just selling to them.
  • How it sounds: Reassuring and clipped when it counts ("Cancel anytime. No contract. Simple. Fair. Risk-free."). We name what costs extra, we admit where a rival is genuinely good, and we tie hiring to fairness and compliance.
  • Example: "Pay only when a qualified candidate starts an assessment. Cancel anytime — no contract, no conditions."
  • Avoid: Hidden costs, one-sided "we win every category" comparisons, and dark-pattern urgency. Don't oversell; earn the trust.

5. Built to Scale Without Cutting Corners

  • What it means: The same platform serves a 10-person startup and a company hiring 500,000 people a year — and it stays rigorous, integrity-checked, and compliant at every size.
  • How it sounds: Capable and range-aware. We show breadth (SMB → enterprise, 4,500+ roles, 50+ industries, 100+ ATS integrations) while insisting on quality, anti-cheating, and a defensible process.
  • Example: "Whether you're making your first ten hires or your ten-thousandth, Testlify keeps every assessment consistent, proctored, and defensible."
  • Avoid: Implying scale means sloppiness, or that speed and volume come at the cost of quality or fairness.

Tone Guidelines

General Tone: The Confident Hiring Partner

Write like an experienced talent-assessment expert sitting next to a recruiter who's up against a deadline. You know the data cold, you genuinely want their next hire to work out, and you explain the how without ever making them feel behind. Confident but never arrogant; warm but never fluffy; always ready with a number or a next step.

Tone Variations by Content Type

How-To Guides: Instructive, reassuring, step-by-step, second person.

  • "Read on to find answers to all your questions on this."
  • "Not sure where to begin? Start here."
  • "The talent review process doesn't have to be a chore, though it does require planning."

Strategy/Advice Content: Authoritative, data-backed, "steal the playbook."

  • "But, here's the catch: here's how they actually do it — so your company can too."
  • "Here's what high-volume recruiting teams do differently."
  • "In a world without linear career paths, you must focus on assessing and fostering real skills."

Industry News/Trends: Insightful, analytical, forward-looking.

  • "But why is this trend emerging now? Here's what's really driving it."
  • "This shift means recruiters need to rethink how they screen."
  • "Here's why this matters for your next hiring cycle."

Product/Feature Content: Benefit-led, clear, proof-backed, honest.

  • "Everything you need to assess, interview, and hire — in one platform."
  • "Reduce time-to-hire by 55% and cut hiring costs by 72%."
  • "Made by humans, for humans — get polished, professional results in seconds."

Messaging Framework

Core Brand Messages

Message 1: Hire the Best, Not Just Fast

  • Concept: The point of good hiring isn't speed for its own sake — it's consistently landing the right person.
  • Key Points:
    • Quality of hire is the real metric, and it's measurable.
    • Speed and cost savings (55% faster, 72% cheaper) come because the process is rigorous, not instead of it.
    • A bad hire is expensive — up to ~30% of the role's salary.
  • Usage: Homepage, top-of-funnel positioning, brand-level messaging.

Message 2: Skills Over Resumes

  • Concept: Assess what candidates can actually do — objectively, fairly, without bias.
  • Key Points:
    • Resumes and interviews are gameable; validated skills assessment isn't.
    • Objective scoring removes bias and "gut feel," and it's more defensible.
    • Works across cognitive, technical, personality, situational, and role-specific skills.
  • Usage: Skills-based-hiring content, DEI/fairness topics, competitor comparisons.

Message 3: Everything in One Platform

  • Concept: Assess, interview, proctor, and integrate — without stitching tools together.
  • Key Points:
    • 3,500+ ready-to-use tests across 4,500+ roles and 50+ industries.
    • Conversational AI interviews in chat, voice, and video that simulate real on-the-job scenarios.
    • 20+ anti-cheating/proctoring measures and 100+ ATS integrations out of the box.
  • Usage: Product pages, feature content, capability and scalability discussions.

Message 4: Fair, Transparent, Low-Risk

  • Concept: Pricing and assessment you can trust — no hidden costs, no lock-in, no bias.
  • Key Points:
    • Credit-based: pay only when a qualified candidate starts an assessment.
    • 30-day money-back guarantee, 24-month price lock, cancel anytime.
    • Bias-free, SOC 2 Type II / ISO 27001 / GDPR / CCPA compliant, defensible.
  • Usage: Pricing, plan-comparison, enterprise/compliance, and trust-building content.

Message 5: Scales From Startup to Enterprise

  • Concept: One platform that grows with you — from your first hires to hiring at massive volume.
  • Key Points:
    • Trusted by 1,500+ teams worldwide, from SMBs to large enterprises.
    • Handles high-volume and mass recruitment without losing rigor.
    • Enterprise-grade security, proctoring, and a defensible selection process.
  • Usage: Enterprise, high-volume hiring, and scalability content.

Writing Style Guidelines

Sentence Structure

  • Vary length: Mix short, punchy lines ("Simple. Fair. Risk-free.") with longer explanatory sentences.
  • Active voice preferred: "Testlify scores every candidate against the same criteria" — not "Candidates are scored by Testlify."
  • Average length: ~15–22 words per sentence; lead with the benefit, then the mechanism.
  • Clarity first: If a sentence needs a second read, rewrite it. Plain beats clever.

Paragraph Structure

  • Length: 2–4 sentences; one idea per paragraph.
  • One idea per paragraph: Each block makes a single point the reader can skim and grasp.
  • Transitions: Use rhetorical questions and colon-led lead-ins to move the reader forward.
  • White space: Break up long blocks; favor scannable structure over dense prose.

Word Choice

  • Second person: Write to "you" and "your team," not about "the recruiter."
  • Specific over vague: "Reduce time-to-hire by 55%" — not "hire faster."
  • Active verbs: Assess, Evaluate, Screen, Hire, Identify, Streamline, Build, Verify.
  • Cut hype and AI-clichés: Avoid "seamless," "leverage," "robust," "unlock," "delve," "elevate," "revolutionary," and empty intensifiers ("very," "really," "truly").

Terminology

Say This → Not That

  • Skills assessment / talent assessment → Test (positions the platform, not a single quiz)
  • Skills-based hiring → Resume screening (it's the core thesis; resumes are what we move beyond)
  • Assess / evaluate → Guess ("assess, don't guess" is the through-line)
  • Candidates → Applicants (candidate is the term used site-wide)
  • Conversational AI interview → Chatbot (it simulates real on-the-job scenarios, not small talk)
  • Time-to-hire / quality of hire → "efficiency" (name the specific metric)

Content Formatting

Headlines

  • Be specific: "How to Perform a Talent Review in 2026 [in 5 Simple Steps]" — not "Talent Review Tips."
  • Include the benefit or the number: "Top 15 [X] Alternatives in 2026," "Reduce Time-to-Hire by 55%."
  • Use questions: Reader-search-shaped headers ("How does Walmart hire at scale?") pull people in.
  • Keep it scannable: Front-load the keyword; keep titles tight enough for SEO.

Subheadings

  • Descriptive and question-shaped: A reader should grasp the whole article from the H2s alone.
  • Scannable: Support skimming — each subhead is a self-contained promise.
  • Keyword-rich: Include the target term naturally.
  • Parallel structure: Keep the pattern consistent within a piece (e.g., all "What is…/Why…/How…").

Lists

  • Numbered for sequence/ranking: Steps, "Top 15," and staged processes.
  • Bulleted with bold lead-ins for features/benefits: "Anti-cheating: 20+ proctoring measures…"
  • Comparison tables and pros/cons blocks: Use for "vs" pages, pricing, and plan comparisons.
  • Parallel and consistent: Every bullet starts the same way (verb, or bold label + colon).

Calls-to-Action

  • Use the real, primary CTAs: "Try for Free" and "Book a Demo" (also "Book a free 30-minute live demo," "View plans").
  • Clear action + value: Tell the reader what they get, not just "Learn more."
  • Match intensity to the stage: Soft in-content CTAs on blogs; direct trial/demo CTAs on product pages.
  • Place naturally: CTAs flow at the end of a value-delivering section, never mid-thought.

Voice Examples

Excellent Testlify Voice ✅

"But, here's the catch: Walmart isn't just the world's largest retailer; it's also #1 on the Fortune Global 500 and hires over 500,000 people a year.

How does a company hiring at that scale stay #1?

In this blog, we break down the Walmart interview process so your company can do it too. Because skills-based, automated hiring at volume isn't a Walmart-only trick — it's a practice any recruiter from any company can adopt."

Why this works:

  • Opens on a concrete, specific hook (real numbers), not a generic intro.
  • Speaks directly to "your company" — the reader is in the story.
  • Confident and data-backed without hype.
  • Promises a practical, usable takeaway ("so your company can do it too").
  • Reinforces the skills-based thesis in plain language.

Not Testlify Voice ❌

(Constructed anti-example — not from any Testlify page.)

"In today's rapidly evolving talent landscape, Testlify offers a comprehensive, best-in-class suite of synergistic assessment solutions engineered to holistically revolutionize your end-to-end recruitment paradigm.

Our robust, AI-powered platform leverages cutting-edge technology to seamlessly unlock unprecedented hiring efficiencies across the entire candidate lifecycle.

Click here to learn more about our innovative features!"

Why this fails:

  • Buzzword soup ("synergistic," "holistically," "paradigm") that says nothing concrete.
  • AI-cliché words we ban ("robust," "leverages," "seamlessly," "unlock," "revolutionize").
  • Features-and-adjectives dump with zero real numbers or proof.
  • No "you," no reader problem, no practical takeaway.
  • Generic "Click here to learn more!" CTA with no value.

Audience Understanding

Who We Write For

Primary Audience: Recruiters, Talent-Acquisition & HR teams

  • Range from SMBs and startups to large enterprises.
  • Own the outcome of every hire and feel the cost of a bad one.
  • Under time pressure, often screening high volumes of candidates.
  • Want objective, fair, defensible decisions — not gut feel.
  • Value practical, usable advice over theory.

Secondary Audiences:

  • CXOs / Business Leaders: Care about quality of hire, cost, and hiring at scale.
  • Enterprise Compliance & Federal Contractors: Need adverse-impact monitoring and a defensible, documented process.
  • HRtech Resellers: Partners reselling assessment capability.
  • Career Counselors / Educators: Reference our glossaries and skills content.

What They Care About

Top Priorities:

  1. Quality of hire — landing the right person, consistently.
  2. Speed — reducing time-to-hire without lowering the bar.
  3. Cost — hiring efficiently and proving ROI.
  4. Fairness — bias-free, objective, defensible selection.
  5. Scale — handling volume and growth without chaos.

Pain Points:

  1. Bad hires that cost ~30% of salary (or ~$15k) and disrupt teams.
  2. Resume manipulation and interview cheating hiding true ability.
  3. Slow processes and candidate drop-off from clunky hiring.
  4. Unconscious bias and legal/adverse-impact exposure.
  5. Choosing between tools, budgets, and stitched-together systems.

How to Serve Them

  • Lead with proof: Open with the number or the study, then the claim.
  • Be specific and usable: Give steps and a "what to look for," not platitudes.
  • Acknowledge the grind: Show we know hiring is hard, high-stakes work.
  • Stay skills-first and fair: Tie advice back to objective, unbiased assessment.
  • Respect their time: Get to the point; make every section skimmable.

Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

  • [ ] Voice: Sounds like Testlify — confident, proof-backed, helpful, skills-first.
  • [ ] Tone: Right for the content type and the reader's stage.
  • [ ] Value: Gives recruiters and HR teams genuine, usable insight.
  • [ ] Proof: Claims are backed by a real number or a cited source.
  • [ ] Clarity: Complex ideas explained plainly; no jargon dumps.
  • [ ] Specificity: Concrete metrics and examples, not vague filler.
  • [ ] Fairness: Reflects objective, bias-free, defensible hiring.
  • [ ] Action: Clear next step or takeaway the reader can use.
  • [ ] Terminology: Uses the preferred hiring/assessment terms.
  • [ ] No AI-clichés: None of the banned hype words slipped in.

Remember: Every piece of content should help a recruiter or HR team hire the best person — on evidence, not guesswork. We're not just publishing information; we're making better, fairer hiring decisions possible.
