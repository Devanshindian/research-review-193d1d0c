What the winners cover

Confirmed format:

  • Explainer-led how-to guide with definition, benefits, implementation steps, and occasional comparison; deep — TestGorilla ~7.5k words, SHRM ~7.4k words, AIHR ~5.9k words.

Common H2s (most competitors have):

  • What skills-based hiring is
  • Why employers use it and its benefits
  • How it differs from traditional hiring
  • Implementation steps
  • Hiring outcomes, including quality, diversity, and retention

Where the winners drift:

  • TestGorilla pivots into a recruiter-focused segment for tech and finance, while SHRM frames the topic partly as a workplace-trend news story.

Gaps we can own:

  • Annual benchmark reporting rather than general guidance or product-led implementation content
  • Assessment-stage metrics: candidate drop-off, time spent in tests, and candidate NPS
  • Quality-of-hire comparisons by screening method
  • A free, ungated benchmark report rather than a lead-capture model
  • Year-over-year evidence on how skills-based screening performs in practice

Build spec

  • Word band: 4,000-6,000 words for a data-led annual benchmark report, with charts and tables carrying findings rather than padded commentary.
  • Structure: Put skills based hiring in a benefit-led H1 of 60 characters or fewer, within the first 100 words, in 2-3 H2s, and in the conclusion.
  • Structure: Use 4-7 H2s, with at least half phrased as questions; lead every section with a self-contained 40-60 word answer that can stand alone in search or AI results.
  • Featured-snippet target: Open with a 40-60 word definition of skills-based hiring that explains assessment of demonstrated capabilities rather than degrees, titles, or years of experience.
  • Primary sources to cite: Use Testlify's own anonymised benchmark dataset for report findings, then cite the EEOC on employment tests and selection procedures, SIOP's selection-validation principles, the U.S. Department of Labor's OFCCP guidance on disparate impact, and BLS JOLTS data for turnover context.
  • Close: End with the next annual benchmark update and one Testlify CTA: benchmark your assessment funnel and screening method against the report's findings.
