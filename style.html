Style & mechanics

Testlify Style Guide

Grammar & Mechanics

Capitalization

  • Headlines & subheadings: Sentence case — capitalize only the first word and proper nouns ("What are pink-collar jobs?", "Why is DEI so important?"). Exception: role names in interview-question listicles are Title-Cased because the role is a proper noun ("60 Maintenance Technician interview questions").
  • Product names: "Testlify" is always capitalized, one word, never stylized; possessive "Testlify's". Features are written descriptively in lowercase (skills assessments, conversational AI interviews, test library, proctoring); named tests/frameworks are Title-Cased (Cognitive Ability Test, Candidate Data Profile (CDP)). Competitors keep their native casing (TestGorilla, Adaface, Mercer Mettl, iMocha, HireVue, HackerRank).
  • Industry terms (house spelling/casing — lowercase in body): skills assessment · skills-based hiring · candidate · recruiter · hiring manager · talent acquisition · time-to-hire · time-to-fill · cost-per-hire · quality of hire · aptitude test · situational judgment test (SJT) · job description / job specification · culture fit · pre-employment testing · onboarding · power skills (Testlify's term for soft skills) · blue-/white-/pink-collar (hyphenated as adjectives) · 360-degree feedback · 9-box grid · candidate experience · proctoring · high-volume hiring · structured interview · unconscious bias · employer brand. (Fix current inconsistencies: always hyphenate "pink-collar"; keep metric names lowercase in body — don't Title-Case "time-to-fill" in FAQs.)

Numbers

  • Spell out one–nine; use numerals for 10 and above in prose — but the blogs (and our house style) use numerals for all stats and data, so: always numerals for percentages (40%), money ($4,700; use ₹ for India figures), measurements, ranges (2–4 weeks; 15% to 30%), and list/step counts (7 interview questions, Step 1).
  • Large numbers: use commas (1,000+); spell out "million"/"billion" (2.1 million). (Numerals dominate the blogs — when in doubt for a figure/stat, use the numeral.)

Punctuation

  • Oxford comma: Yes — apply consistently ("reliability, adaptability, and safety").
  • Em dashes: rare in the blogs and inconsistently rendered — standardize: use an em dash sparingly for an aside (closed, no spaces); use hyphens for compound modifiers (skills-based, role-specific, data-driven) and ranges.
  • Quotation marks: curly — curly double for quotations/pull quotes, curly single for scare-quoted terms; curly apostrophes throughout.
  • Ellipses: three dots, no surrounding spaces, used sparingly (mainly for omitted text in quotes). In practice the blogs use essentially none — avoid ellipses for stylistic pauses (the few present are accidental double-periods).

Abbreviations & Acronyms

  • Spell out on first use, then use the acronym: "Applicant Tracking System (ATS)".
  • Common industry acronyms (define on first use): ATS, HR, HRIS, DEI, KPI, SMART, SJT, EEOC, ROI, NPS, CI/CD (plus AI, SEO, IQ, EQ, STEM, SHRM, CXO as they appear). (First-use expansion is inconsistent in current blogs — enforce it.)
  • Latin abbreviations: use "e.g.", "i.e.", "etc." sparingly.

Word Choice & Usage

Preferred Terms — "Say This → Not That"

  • candidates → not "applicants"
  • power skills (Testlify's term) → alongside "soft skills"
  • skills-based hiring → not "credential-based hiring"
  • culture fit → pick one and be consistent (not alternating "cultural fit"/"cultural fitness")
  • Prefer: structured interview · role-specific tests · objectively evaluate · reduce bias · make informed hiring decisions · screen out / shortlist · high-volume hiring
  • Attribute every stat to a named source ("According to SHRM…", "A McKinsey study found…")
  • [COMPANY] — confirm product-naming and positioning language with marketing (exact feature names, when to say "AI interviews" vs "conversational AI interviews").

Words to Avoid

  • Cut filler: "very", "really", "actually" (usually unnecessary).
  • No "click here" / "read more" — use descriptive link text.
  • No AI-clichés / hype (all observed in current blogs — cut): "in today's rapidly evolving / fast-paced landscape", ever-evolving, delve, dive into, navigate through, unlock the potential/power, harness the power of, seamless, streamline (overused), robust, holistic, comprehensive, at scale, "hiring game", journey, empower, cornerstone of success, beacon of hope, linchpin, needle in a haystack, take to the next level, win-win, future-proofing, elevate, ripple effect, "no easy feat", "the secret to", "there you have it", "But don't worry!", "in short"/"in essence", "it's not just X; it's Y".
  • Don't over-open with "However / Additionally / Moreover"; drop the "Remember," paragraph-opener tic.

Inclusive Language

  • Gender-neutral where possible; people-first language; avoid idioms that don't translate.

Formatting Standards

Text Formatting

  • Bold for genuine emphasis (sparingly); italics for terms/titles; no underline (reads as a link); no ALL CAPS.

Lists

  • Bulleted for non-sequential items; numbered for steps/rankings; nested lists indented one level; items parallel; one idea each.

Links

  • Descriptive anchor text (never "click here"); link the phrase that names the destination.

Code & Technical Elements

  • inline code for short snippets / UI labels; fenced blocks for multi-line code.

Callout Boxes / Asides

  • Use sparingly for tips, warnings, or key takeaways.

Content Structure

Article Introduction

  • Hook → the reader's problem → the promise of the article (≈150–250 words). (Answer-first intro + TL;DR specifics live in seo-aeo-geo-guidelines.md.)

Section Length

  • One idea per section; ~200–300 words; clear H2/H3 hierarchy; scannable.

Conclusion

  • Forward-looking next step + one clear CTA (≈150–200 words). (No recap — see seo-aeo-geo-guidelines.md.)

SEO-Specific Style

Meta Titles

  • ≤ 60 characters, primary keyword near the front.

Meta Descriptions

  • ~140–160 characters, includes the keyword, reads as a benefit.

URL Slugs

  • Short, lowercase, hyphenated, keyword-focused; no stop-words / dates.

Alt Text

  • Describe the image; include the keyword only when natural.

Dates & Time

  • Format: "Month DD, YYYY" (e.g. July 5, 2026). Consistent, unambiguous formats; avoid "5/4".

Statistics & Data

Citing Sources

  • Cite the source inline and link it; name the source; don't invent or misround. (The blogs already attribute to SHRM, McKinsey, Gartner — keep that habit.)

Presenting Numbers

  • Numerals for scannability; keep units consistent.

Images & Media

Image Captions

  • Where they add context; brief and specific.

Screenshots

  • Current, legible, annotated where helpful.

Charts & Graphs

  • Labelled axes/units and a clear title; source cited.

Brand-Specific Guidelines

Testlify Product References

  • "Testlify" is always one word, capitalized. Describe features in lowercase (skills assessments, conversational AI interviews, test library, proctoring); Title-Case named products/tests. Link to the relevant product/test page when naming a specific test. — confirm exact naming with marketing.

Competitor References

  • Name competitors in their native casing; compare fairly and factually, no disparagement. Per seo-aeo-geo-guidelines.md, never cite or link a direct assessment competitor as a source (Adaface, Criteria, eSkill, iMocha, Mettl, TestDome, TestGorilla, Vervoe, WeCP) — critique their approaches generically. — confirm any do/don't rules with marketing.

Accessibility

Screen Reader Friendly

  • Real heading hierarchy, alt text on images, descriptive link text.

Plain Language

  • Short sentences; define jargon on first use.

Voice & Tone Reminders

Core Voice Characteristics

  • Observed from the blogs: confident, second-person ("you/your"), proof-backed (named-source stats), plain-spoken, practical/how-to. The tighter recent blogs — short sentences, plain verbs ("Start with the work, not the wish list") — are the target register. (The full voice lives in brand-voice.md.)

Tone Variations

  • How tone shifts by content type — see brand-voice.md (Tone Guidelines).

Editing Checklist

Before publishing: spelling/grammar checked · punctuation consistent (Oxford comma, dashes, curly quotes) · numbers & dates consistent · preferred terminology used · formatting consistent · headings hierarchical · strong intro + CTA · keyword integrated naturally · meta + slug + alt text done · internal/external links working · sources cited (named + linked) · genuinely useful · ready to publish.

Updates & Maintenance

  • Keep the guide current: add new terms/acronyms as they appear, revisit when products or positioning change, and keep it aligned with brand-voice.md.
